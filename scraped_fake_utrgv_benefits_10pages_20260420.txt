Honors Research and Study Travel Mini-Grant | UTRGV Student Success Skip to main content Students Faculty Staff Locations myUTRGV Blackboard Search Website Apply Now Academics Student Services Campus Life About UTRGV Student Success Financial Support Honors Opportunities Research Travel Mini-Grant

The Honors Research and Study Travel Mini-Grant helps current UTRGV Honors College students pay for approved academic travel. Funds may be used for study abroad deposits, undergraduate research conferences, pre-health enrichment programs, or travel connected to a faculty-approved academic project.

Award amount: up to $1,250 per academic year.
Eligible students must be enrolled full-time at The University of Texas Rio Grande Valley, be active in the Honors College, have a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.50, and submit a short travel purpose statement before travel begins. Students may be Texas residents or out-of-state students. Financial need is considered but is not required.

Priority consideration is given to first-year and sophomore Honors students who are building an academic plan for graduate school, medical school, law school, or other professional programs.

Students who already receive merit scholarships may still apply. National Merit recipients are eligible as long as the proposed travel is academic and not personal vacation travel.

This page also includes information about the Honors Travel Planning Workshop. The workshop is a planning event only. Attending the workshop does not guarantee funding and is not itself a scholarship.

How to apply: sign in with your UTRGV student email, upload your travel estimate, and request a faculty or Honors advisor recommendation.

Questions: honors-support@utrgv.edu

Childcare Access Grant for Student Parents | UTRGV Dean of Students Skip to main content Students Faculty Staff Locations myUTRGV Blackboard Search Website Apply Now Academics Student Services Campus Life About UTRGV Dean of Students Student Parent Resources Childcare Access Grant

The Childcare Access Grant is designed to help enrolled student parents remain in class when childcare costs create a barrier to attendance. The grant may be used for licensed childcare, after-school care, approved campus childcare partners, or short-term emergency care while the student attends class, lab, advising, tutoring, or exams.

Award amount: up to $900 per semester.
Eligible students must be currently enrolled at UTRGV, have at least one dependent child, have a completed FAFSA on file, and demonstrate financial need. Students with a Student Aid Index of 0, Pell Grant eligibility, or documented low-income status receive priority. Undergraduate and graduate students may apply. Full-time enrollment is not required, but students must be enrolled in at least 6 credit hours.

This grant is not available to students who do not have dependents. It cannot be used to pay a relative who does not operate as an approved childcare provider.

Students may combine this grant with scholarships, work-study, emergency aid, and food pantry support.

The Student Parent Study Circle listed on this page is a support meeting and does not provide money. The free calendar template and parenting newsletter are informational resources only.

How to apply: complete the Student Parent Resource form, upload proof of dependent status, and submit a childcare cost estimate.

Questions: studentparents@utrgv.edu

The Pre-Health Shadowing and MCAT Support Program connects qualified UTRGV students with physician shadowing opportunities, basic clinical observation training, and a limited number of MCAT preparation vouchers. The program is intended for students exploring medical school, dental school, physician assistant programs, or other health professional pathways.

Benefits may include:
MCAT preparation voucher up to $600.
Clinical observation placement assistance.
HIPAA and patient privacy training at no cost.
A letter of participation for professional school applications.

Eligible students must be enrolled at UTRGV, be in a pre-medical or health-professions advising track, or be majoring in Biology, Biomedical Sciences, Chemistry, or a related pre-health field. Applicants must have a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.40. First-year students are encouraged to apply if they have completed at least one semester or are enrolled in a first-year Honors or pre-health cohort.

Students must show proof of current health insurance before beginning any in-person shadowing placement. Students who do not have insurance may still attend the online information sessions, but they cannot be placed in a clinical site until proof of coverage is submitted.

The Pre-Health Pizza Social, resume tips page, and medical school myths handout are not funding programs. They are informational events and resources.

How to apply: submit the pre-health interest form using your UTRGV student email and attach an unofficial transcript.

Questions: prehealth@utrgv.edu

Out-of-State Merit Waiver for National Merit Students | UTRGV Scholarships Skip to main content Students Faculty Staff Locations myUTRGV Blackboard Search Website Apply Now Academics Student Services Campus Life About Scholarships Tuition Support Out-of-State Merit Waiver

The Out-of-State Merit Waiver for National Merit Students reduces the nonresident tuition rate for high-achieving undergraduates who enroll at The University of Texas Rio Grande Valley. The waiver is intended to help out-of-state students continue at UTRGV after receiving a major merit award.

Eligible students must be classified as out-of-state for tuition purposes, be enrolled full-time as an undergraduate, have a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.50, and hold a recognized national merit scholarship, National Merit Finalist award, or comparable university-approved merit award. New freshmen may be considered using high school merit documentation until a UTRGV GPA is posted.

Students with high household income may still qualify because this is a merit-based tuition waiver. FAFSA completion is recommended but not required unless the student also wants to be considered for need-based awards.

This waiver is not for Texas residents, international students on temporary visas, or students enrolled only in graduate programs. It is also not a cash refund. It reduces eligible tuition charges on the student account.

The Tuition Estimator linked on this page is a calculator only. It is not a scholarship, grant, or waiver.

How to apply: upload National Merit documentation through the scholarship portal and select Nonresident Merit Waiver Review.

Questions: scholarships@utrgv.edu

The Technology Emergency Loan and Hotspot Program provides temporary laptops, mobile hotspots, webcams, calculators, and charging equipment to enrolled UTRGV students who do not have reliable technology for coursework.

Eligible students must be currently enrolled at UTRGV and explain a technology barrier. Priority is given to students who report a broken or unavailable personal computer, no reliable home internet, Pell Grant eligibility, low-income status, or use of library computers as their primary coursework device. Students in online, hybrid, STEM, writing-intensive, and senior project courses may receive priority when equipment is limited.

Loan period: up to one semester.
Cost: no rental fee when equipment is returned on time.
Available support: laptop checkout, hotspot checkout, basic software setup, and referral to free Microsoft Office and other campus software.

Students who already own a working laptop and have reliable internet may still use campus computer labs, but they are lower priority for emergency equipment loans.

The Digital Study Skills Badge listed on this page is a free training module. It is not a cash benefit. Completing the badge does not guarantee a laptop.

How to apply: submit the technology hardship request, choose pickup location, and bring a valid student ID.
